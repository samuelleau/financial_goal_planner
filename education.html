Financial Basics for Gen Z

Emergency Fund

Your financial safety net - aim for 3-6 months of expenses

Steps to Build:

  1. Calculate monthly expenses
  2. Set initial goal of $1,000
  3. Automate $50-100/month savings
  4. Keep in high-yield savings account
  5. Gradually increase to 3-6 months
Start small - even $25/week adds up to $1,300/year!

Building Credit

Establish good credit early for better financial opportunities

Credit Building Strategy:

  1. Get a student or secured credit card
  2. Keep utilization below 30% (ideally under 10%)
  3. Pay full balance every month
  4. Never miss payment due dates
  5. Monitor credit score monthly
Set up autopay for minimum payments to never miss a due date!

The 50/30/20 Rule

Simple budgeting framework for balanced financial health

50% Needs Rent, groceries, utilities, minimum debt payments
30% Wants Entertainment, dining out, hobbies, subscriptions
20% Savings & Debt Emergency fund, investments, extra debt payments

Irregular Income Budgeting

Perfect for gig workers, freelancers, and part-time jobs

Strategy:

  1. Calculate lowest monthly income from past 6 months
  2. Budget based on this minimum amount
  3. Treat extra income as "bonus" for goals
  4. Build larger emergency fund (6+ months)
  5. Use percentage-based budgeting

Two proven strategies to eliminate debt faster

  1. List all debts by interest rate (highest first)
  2. Pay minimums on all debts
  3. Put extra money toward highest rate debt
  4. Repeat until debt-free

Best for: Saving the most money on interest

  4. Celebrate wins and build momentum

Avoiding Debt Traps

Common pitfalls and how to avoid them

Minimum Payment Trap: Only paying minimums keeps you in debt forever
Lifestyle Inflation: Spending more as income increases
Buy Now, Pay Later: Easy to overspend with delayed payments
Payday Loans: Extremely high interest rates (avoid at all costs)

Advanced Wealth Building

FIRE Movement

Financial Independence, Retire Early

Lean FIRE: $1M saved, $40K/year expenses
Regular FIRE: $2.5M saved, $100K/year expenses
Fat FIRE: $5M+ saved, $200K+/year expenses

Path to FIRE:

  1. Calculate your FIRE number (25x annual expenses)
  2. Maximize savings rate (50%+ for early retirement)
  3. Invest in low-cost index funds
  4. Optimize taxes (401k, IRA, HSA)
  5. Consider side hustles for extra income
